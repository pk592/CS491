Proper Shaking Techniques

Below, are the main shaking techniques used in bartending

  • Wet Shaking: A wet shake usually follows from a dry shake and as the name implies, consists of shaking the ingredients with ice in a cocktail shaker before straining into a glass.
  • Dry Shaking: A dry shake is a cocktail technique that involves shaking the ingredients without adding ice to the shaker. It usually precedes a wet shake where the ingredients are shaken with ice.
  • Rolling: Rolling is used to to chill and aerate a cocktail while combining thicker ingredients together.
